abstract: "It has long been recognized that the \u201CIrish Question\u201D was also\
  \ an imperial question. The vast Irish diaspora in the settler colonies ensured\
  \ that Home Rule had enormous consequences for the wider empire. But scholars have\
  \ yet fully to appreciate the part that political elites in the self-governing Dominions\
  \ played in this story. This article explores the role of colonial statesmen in\
  \ Anglo-Irish affairs. Figures like Australia\u2019s Billy Hughes or South Africa\u2019\
  s Jan Smuts were able to navigate the emotional complexities of Irish nationalist\
  \ politics in a manner that transcended British party politics. In the process,\
  \ they framed \u201Ccolonial\u201D Home Rule as a compromise between British rule\
  \ and independence. This article shows how Irish nationalist politics became enmeshed\
  \ with imperial politics in a manner that blurred the line between the local, national,\
  \ imperial, and global."
