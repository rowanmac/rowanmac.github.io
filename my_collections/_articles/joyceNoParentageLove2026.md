abstract: "This essay considers a strain of queer nationalist thinking that was central\
  \ to the 1916 Easter Rising in Ireland through and against the more familiar model\
  \ associated with Oscar Wilde. Where Wilde's politics were grounded in Oxford Hellenism's\
  \ advocacy of spiritual procreation (in a discursive space that, as Linda Dowling\
  \ has suggested, was opened by Malthusian projections of overcrowding), nationalists\
  \ such as Patrick Pearse and Roger Casement traced a different trajectory. Post-Famine\
  \ underpopulation demanded a regenerative approach in which the emphasis falls upon\
  \ the reiterative production of rebellious Irish subjects, one I suggest was equally\u2014\
  but differently\u2014queer as Wilde's."
