abstract: "Partition was about minorities and their oppression \u2013 real or imagined,\
  \ anticipated or remembered \u2013 which inspired a wide debate, still relevant\
  \ today for the future of Northern Ireland. The partitionist assumptions \u2013\
  \ that a new nation-state required religious homogeneity and that minorities would\
  \ be victimised \u2013 were rooted in historical experience and reflected contemporary\
  \ political practice. This book illuminates the historical significance of religious\
  \ minorities in southern Ireland at a time when the twenty-six Counties formed 'a\
  \ Catholic state for a Catholic people'. Dragged into a process of nation building\
  \ about which Jews and Protestants had serious reservations, they often felt like\
  \ guests of an unappeasable host. Many emigrated, but those who stayed offered a\
  \ critical contribution to civil society. Based on a wide range of primary sources,\
  \ including recently discovered personal diaries, Eugenio F. Biagini's holistic\
  \ account of the minority experience explains the role of entrenched diversity in\
  \ shaping attitudes to civil rights and national identity."
