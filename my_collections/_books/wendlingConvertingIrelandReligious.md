abstract: "Karina B\xE9nazech Wendling offers a re-assessment of \u2018souperism\u2019\
  \u2015the long-debated claim that food was used to convert Irish Catholics to Protestantism\
  \ during the Great Famine. Focusing on the Irish Society for Promoting the Education\
  \ of the Native Irish through their Own Language, the first group labeled \u2018\
  soupers\u2019 in 1841, she uncovers a more complex picture. Rather than a mere tool\
  \ of British cultural imperialism, the Society had a deep engagement with the Irish\
  \ language and Bible translation, while also encouraging religious conversions in\
  \ the West. The book explores the Society\u2019s role in Ireland\u2019s religious\
  \ and political landscape, the rise of Catholic counter-missions, and nationalist\
  \ resistance. Offering fresh insights into Ireland\u2019s religious history and\
  \ global missionary movements, this book is essential for scholars of Irish studies,\
  \ interdenominational relations, and education in Ireland. -- ."
