abstract: "County Wicklow\u2019s landscape is dotted with pre-Christian sites \u2013\
  \ Baltinglass is known as the \u2018Hillfort Capital of Ireland\u2019. Saint Patrick\
  \ landed in Wicklow in 432 AD and the county also boasts ecclesiastical sites, the\
  \ most impressive being Glendalough. Following the Anglo-Norman invasion of 1169\u2013\
  70, Normans occupied the lowlands, but native Irish inhabited the uplands, leading\
  \ to conflict throughout the Middle Ages. Wicklow became the last county to be shired\
  \ in 1606.Wicklow witnessed more violence through the seventeenth century, and it\
  \ was only after 1700 that the elite felt safe enough to build great houses such\
  \ as Powerscourt and Russborough. Wicklow was in turmoil during the 1798 rebellion.\
  \ Economic recovery was halted by the tragedy of Famine. Later in the nineteenth\
  \ century, the Parnells led the Home Rule movement, the Land League and the Ladies\u2019\
  \ Land League. The twentieth century saw war, revolution and hardship before better\
  \ times arrived after 1960.Meticulously researched, this clear, user-friendly book\
  \ is an invaluable resource which will appeal to everyone interested in the history\
  \ of County Wicklow."
